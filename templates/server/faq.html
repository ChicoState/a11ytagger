Frequently Asked Questions

What is a11ytagger?

a11ytagger is a web-based tool designed to help you add accessibility tags to PDF documents. It automates the process of making PDFs accessible to users who rely on screen readers and other assistive technologies.

Why is PDF accessibility important?

Accessible PDFs ensure that everyone, including people with disabilities, can access and understand your content. Properly tagged PDFs work with screen readers, allow for better navigation, and comply with accessibility standards like WCAG and Section 508.

How do I use a11ytagger?

Simply upload your PDF document through our interface. a11ytagger will analyze the document, identify accessibility issues, and help you add the necessary structure tags. Once processed, you can download the accessible version of your PDF.

What file formats are supported?

Currently, a11ytagger supports PDF files. We're working on expanding support for additional document formats in future releases.

Is my document data secure?

Yes. Your uploaded documents are processed securely and are not stored permanently on our servers. Files are temporarily cached during processing and are automatically removed after your session ends.

Do I need to create an account?

No account is required to use a11ytagger. You can upload and process documents immediately without any registration.

What accessibility standards does a11ytagger follow?

a11ytagger helps ensure your PDFs comply with WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) and Section 508 standards by adding proper structure tags, alternative text for images, and logical reading order.

Can I contribute to the project?

Absolutely! a11ytagger is an open-source project. Visit our GitHub repository to learn how you can contribute code, report issues, or suggest new features.

Who developed a11ytagger?

a11ytagger is developed and maintained by students and faculty at California State University, Chico. Learn more about our team on the About page.

Is a11ytagger free to use?

Yes, a11ytagger is completely free to use. As an open-source project, we're committed to making accessibility tools available to everyone at no cost.
